# User Personas

## Purpose

This document describes the different types of users who will use Atlas.

Understanding our users helps us build features that solve real problems instead of making assumptions.

---

# Persona 1 — Organization Administrator

## Overview

The Organization Administrator manages the entire workspace.

This is usually the primary decision-maker and the person responsible for keeping records up to date.

### Examples

- Church Administrator
- School Administrator
- NGO Manager
- Operations Manager

---

## Goals

- Keep organization records organized
- Monitor attendance
- Assign follow-ups
- Generate reports
- Manage users
- Track growth

---

## Pain Points

- Too many spreadsheets
- Paper attendance
- Missing information
- Difficult reporting
- Manual follow-up

---

## Needs

- Easy dashboard
- Fast search
- Reliable reports
- Team collaboration
- Role management

---

# Persona 2 — Staff Member

## Overview

Staff members perform daily operational work.

Examples include church workers, teachers, customer support staff, or volunteers.

---

## Goals

- Record attendance
- Complete assigned follow-ups
- Update member information
- View assigned tasks

---

## Pain Points

- Forgetting follow-ups
- Difficult communication
- Slow manual processes

---

## Needs

- Simple interface
- Mobile access
- Task reminders
- Quick search

---

# Persona 3 — Team Leader

## Overview

A Team Leader supervises a department or group.

Examples include:

- Ministry Leader
- School Coordinator
- Department Head
- Volunteer Coordinator

---

## Goals

- Monitor team activities
- Track attendance
- Review reports
- Ensure follow-ups are completed

---

## Needs

- Department dashboard
- Team reports
- Activity tracking
- Notifications

---

# Persona 4 — Organization Owner

## Overview

This is the person who pays for the software.

Examples include:

- Senior Pastor
- School Owner
- NGO Director
- Business Owner

---

## Goals

- Understand organizational growth
- Make informed decisions
- Improve efficiency
- Reduce administrative work

---

## Needs

- Executive dashboard
- High-level reports
- Organization health overview
- Financial subscription management

---

# Persona 5 — Volunteer

## Overview

Volunteers help perform specific tasks but usually have limited permissions.

---

## Goals

- Check attendance
- Follow up assigned people
- View assigned events

---

## Needs

- Simple mobile interface
- Limited access
- Easy navigation

---

# Common Needs Across All Users

Every user wants software that is:

- Easy to learn
- Fast
- Reliable
- Mobile-friendly
- Secure
- Simple to navigate

---

# User Frustrations We Want To Eliminate

Users should never have to:

- Search through spreadsheets
- Record attendance twice
- Forget follow-ups
- Ask multiple people for information
- Switch between different applications

---

# Design Principles

When building Atlas, always remember:

- Reduce the number of clicks.
- Make important actions obvious.
- Keep forms simple.
- Show only information users need.
- Prioritize speed over complexity.

---

# Success

A successful user should be able to:

- Log in
- Find what they need
- Complete their task
- Log out

without needing training or assistance.
