# Pricing Strategy

## Purpose

This document defines the pricing model for Atlas.

The objective is to create a pricing structure that is:

- Easy to understand
- Affordable for small organizations
- Scalable for larger organizations
- Sustainable for the business

---

# Pricing Philosophy

Organizations should pay based on the value they receive.

Pricing should never be confusing.

Keep plans simple.

---

# Business Model

Atlas operates as a Software as a Service (SaaS).

Customers pay a recurring subscription.

Available billing cycles:

- Monthly
- Yearly

Annual subscriptions receive a discount.

---

# Free Plan

Designed for individuals and small organizations that want to explore Atlas.

### Includes

- 1 Organization
- Up to 50 People
- Basic Dashboard
- Attendance Tracking
- Event Management
- Basic Reports

### Limitations

- Atlas branding
- Limited storage
- Limited exports
- Community support only

---

# Starter Plan

Best for growing organizations.

### Includes

- Everything in Free
- Up to 500 People
- Unlimited Events
- Follow-up Management
- Journey Tracking
- Advanced Reports
- Email Support

---

# Professional Plan

Designed for established organizations.

### Includes

- Everything in Starter
- Unlimited People
- Multiple Teams
- Custom Roles
- Priority Support
- Advanced Analytics
- Data Export
- Audit Logs

---

# Enterprise Plan

Built for large organizations.

### Includes

- Everything in Professional
- Multiple Branches
- Single Sign-On (SSO)
- API Access
- White Labeling
- Dedicated Support
- SLA
- Custom Integrations

---

# Future Add-ons

Optional premium features:

- SMS Notifications
- WhatsApp Messaging
- AI Insights
- Advanced Automation
- Extra Storage
- Custom Domains
- Additional Admin Seats

---

# Trial

Every new organization receives:

- 14-day free trial
- No credit card required

After the trial:

- Upgrade
- Continue on Free (if eligible)

---

# Billing

Supported billing frequencies:

- Monthly
- Annually

Future:

- Multi-year plans

---

# Discounts

Possible discounts:

- Annual billing
- Educational institutions
- Non-profit organizations
- Early adopters
- Referral rewards

---

# Upgrade Path

Organizations can upgrade at any time.

Changes take effect immediately.

Downgrades take effect at the next billing cycle.

---

# Cancellation Policy

Customers can cancel anytime.

After cancellation:

- Data remains available for a grace period.
- Users can reactivate before permanent deletion.

---

# Revenue Principles

Atlas should prioritize:

- Long-term customer relationships
- Transparent pricing
- Fair value
- Sustainable growth

Avoid hidden fees.

---

# Pricing Review

Pricing should be reviewed periodically based on:

- Customer feedback
- Feature usage
- Market trends
- Infrastructure costs
- Competitive positioning

---

# Success Metrics

The pricing model is successful when:

- Customers understand the plans.
- Free users convert to paid plans.
- Churn remains low.
- Revenue grows sustainably.

---

# Notes

Final pricing amounts will be determined closer to public launch after beta testing and customer validation.

The focus during MVP is delivering value, not maximizing revenue.
