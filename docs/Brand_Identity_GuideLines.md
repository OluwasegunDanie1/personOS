# Brand Identity Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the visual and emotional identity of Atlas.

It ensures every experience—whether inside the product, website, documentation, or marketing—feels like Atlas.

---

# Brand Mission

Atlas helps organizations build stronger relationships by making people management simple, intelligent, and enjoyable.

We don't just track attendance.

We help organizations know their people.

---

# Brand Vision

To become the world's most trusted relationship management platform for organizations.

---

# Brand Values

Atlas is built on five values.

- Trust
- Simplicity
- Community
- Growth
- Excellence

Every design and product decision should reinforce these values.

---

# Brand Personality

Atlas should feel:

- Professional
- Modern
- Friendly
- Calm
- Confident
- Intelligent

Never arrogant.

Never playful.

Never overly corporate.

---

# Brand Voice

Our communication should be:

- Clear
- Human
- Helpful
- Encouraging
- Honest

Avoid technical jargon whenever possible.

---

# Tone of Voice

When speaking to users:

Instead of

> "Authentication failed."

Use

> "We couldn't sign you in. Please check your email and password."

Instead of

> "Error 500."

Use

> "Something went wrong on our side. Please try again in a moment."

---

# Logo Usage

Always use approved logo assets.

Never:

- Stretch the logo
- Rotate the logo
- Change logo colors
- Add shadows
- Add gradients
- Place the logo on busy backgrounds

---

# Color Usage

Use only colors from the official Color System.

Primary Blue should be reserved for:

- Primary buttons
- Links
- Active navigation
- Brand highlights

---

# Typography

Primary Font

Inter

Typography should always follow the Typography System.

Never introduce additional fonts into the product.

---

# Photography Style

Images should feel:

- Authentic
- Diverse
- Bright
- Natural
- Community-focused

Avoid stock photos that feel staged or artificial.

---

# Illustration Style

Illustrations should be:

- Minimal
- Flat
- Friendly
- Geometric

Avoid highly detailed artwork.

---

# Icon Style

Icons should:

- Use a consistent outline style
- Have rounded corners
- Maintain equal stroke width

---

# UI Style

The interface should feel:

- Spacious
- Organized
- Fast
- Focused

Whitespace is part of the design.

Avoid clutter.

---

# Writing Guidelines

Buttons should use action verbs.

Examples

- Create Event
- Add Person
- Save Changes
- Invite Member

Avoid vague labels like:

- Submit
- Continue
- Click Here

---

# Accessibility

Brand consistency includes accessibility.

Ensure:

- High contrast
- Readable typography
- Keyboard accessibility
- Inclusive language

---

# Brand Experience

Every interaction should leave users feeling:

"I trust this platform."

"I know what to do next."

"This product feels thoughtfully designed."

---

# Marketing Principles

Atlas marketing should emphasize:

- Relationships over records
- Growth over administration
- Simplicity over complexity
- Value over features

---

# Social Media

Maintain a consistent visual style across:

- LinkedIn
- X (Twitter)
- Facebook
- Instagram
- YouTube

Use:

- Brand colors
- Official logo
- Consistent typography
- Professional imagery

---

# Documentation

All documentation should:

- Use consistent terminology
- Follow the brand voice
- Match product naming conventions

---

# Success Criteria

The Atlas brand is successful when:

- Users recognize Atlas instantly.
- Every touchpoint feels consistent.
- The product inspires trust.
- The visual identity remains timeless.
- The brand grows without losing its personality.

---

# Final Principle

Atlas is not just software.

Atlas is the operating system for organizations that care about people.
