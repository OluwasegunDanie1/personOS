# Brand Identity

## Purpose

This document defines the approved personality, positioning, voice, emotional direction, and visual identity of Relvio.

The Relvio brand exists to communicate a simple idea:

Organizations are built around people, and every relationship matters.

Relvio should be recognizable not only by its visual identity, but by the clarity, calmness, and human focus of every product experience.

This document defines the approved brand direction.

It is not a brand exploration document.

---

# Brand Name

**Relvio**

Relvio is the approved public product name.

**Atlas** was the internal project codename used during early product planning.

Atlas must not appear in:

- Customer-facing interfaces
- Marketing materials
- App store content
- Notifications
- Emails
- Public documentation
- Support content

Historical project documents may reference Atlas when preserving original project context.

To become the trusted People Operating System organizations rely on to understand, engage, and build stronger relationships with their people.

# Brand Mission

Help organizations manage people, understand journeys, strengthen engagement, and take meaningful action through one simple and intelligent platform.

# Brand Promise

Relvio helps organizations stay connected to every person who matters.

People should not disappear inside spreadsheets, disconnected tools, or forgotten follow-ups.

**Build stronger relationships.**

Relvio is built around the belief that organizations become stronger when they understand and care for the people connected to them.

Technology should help organizations remember people, understand context, and act at the right time.

Relvio turns scattered organizational activity into meaningful relationship context.

Relvio is a **People Operating System for organizations**.

It provides one connected workspace for managing:

- People
- Journeys
- Communities
- Events
- Attendance
- Follow-ups
- Communication
- Organizational insights

Relvio is not simply:

Relvio helps organizations understand:

> What has happened before?

> What needs attention?

> What should happen next?

---

# Voice Principles

## Clear

Use simple language.

Avoid unnecessary technical terms.

### Prefer

> Add a person

### Avoid

> Initialize a new person entity

---

## Helpful

Explain what the user needs to know.

When something fails, help the user understand the next action.

### Prefer

> Unable to save this event. Check your connection and try again.

### Avoid

> An unexpected error occurred.

---

## Human

Relvio should sound natural without becoming overly casual.

### Prefer

> No people yet.

> Add your first person to start building your community.

### Avoid

> Zero records available.

---

## Calm

Do not create unnecessary urgency.

Use warning language only when the situation genuinely requires attention.

---

## Concise

Use the shortest sentence that communicates the required meaning clearly.

Avoid long explanations inside the application interface.

Photography Direction

When photography is required, prefer imagery showing:

Real people
Genuine interaction
Communities
Collaboration
Events
Organizational activity

Photography should feel natural and human.

Avoid obviously staged or generic corporate stock photography when possible.

Photography is supporting visual content.

It should not replace the Relvio product identity.

Product Experience

The Relvio product experience should feel:

Fast
Predictable
Calm
Smooth
Helpful

Users should always understand:

Where they are
What is happening
What they can do next

Loading states, empty states, error states, success feedback, and animations are part of the product experience.

Animations should support understanding.

They must not distract from the user's task.

Brand and Product Consistency

Every Relvio feature, screen, communication, or marketing asset should be evaluated using the following questions:

Is it clear?
Is it simple?
Does it feel human?
Does it build trust?
Does it help the user understand people better?
Does it feel consistent with Relvio?
Does it strengthen the idea of connected organizational relationships?

If the answer is no, the work should be reconsidered.

Brand Guardrails

Do not:

Present Relvio as a church-only product
Describe Relvio only as an attendance application
Use Atlas as the public product name
Introduce unrelated brand colors
Redesign the approved logo during implementation
Use overly technical product language
Overload interfaces with unnecessary information
Add visual decoration without product purpose
