# Competitor Analysis

## Purpose

Understand existing products in the market, identify their strengths and weaknesses, and define how Atlas will differentiate itself.

---

# Direct Competitors

## Church Management Systems

Examples include:

- Planning Center
- ChurchTrac
- Breeze ChMS
- Tithe.ly
- ChurchSuite

### Strengths

- Mature products
- Attendance management
- Giving management
- Volunteer scheduling
- Large customer base

### Weaknesses

- Focused mainly on churches
- Some have outdated interfaces
- Limited flexibility outside church use
- Expensive for small churches

---

## CRM Platforms

Examples include:

- HubSpot
- Salesforce
- Zoho CRM

### Strengths

- Powerful customer management
- Automation
- Reporting
- Integrations

### Weaknesses

- Complex setup
- Designed mainly for sales teams
- Expensive
- Too much for small organizations

---

## Event Platforms

Examples include:

- Eventbrite
- TicketTailor
- Meetup

### Strengths

- Event registration
- Ticketing
- Event promotion

### Weaknesses

- Limited people management
- Weak follow-up capabilities
- Not designed for long-term engagement

---

## Productivity Tools

Examples include:

- Notion
- Airtable
- Monday.com
- ClickUp

### Strengths

- Flexible
- Custom workflows
- Collaboration

### Weaknesses

- Require manual setup
- Not purpose-built
- No attendance workflow
- No journey tracking

---

# Indirect Competitors

Organizations also use:

- Excel
- Google Sheets
- Google Forms
- WhatsApp
- Paper records

These are our biggest competitors because they are familiar and free.

---

# What Customers Like About Existing Products

- Easy attendance
- Good reports
- Mobile access
- Email reminders
- Team collaboration

---

# Common Complaints

Users often complain about:

- Too many features
- Difficult setup
- Poor user experience
- High pricing
- Slow support
- Limited customization
- Multiple apps needed for one workflow

---

# Atlas Positioning

Atlas is designed to be:

- Easy to learn
- Easy to set up
- Affordable
- Modern
- Flexible
- Mobile-first

Instead of focusing on one industry, Atlas provides a platform that adapts to different organizations.

---

# Competitive Advantages

Atlas aims to stand out by offering:

- One platform instead of many tools
- Simple user experience
- Flexible journey management
- Built-in follow-up workflows
- Modern dashboard
- Multi-tenant architecture
- Affordable pricing

---

# Areas We Must Win

To compete successfully, Atlas must excel in:

- User experience
- Speed
- Simplicity
- Customer support
- Onboarding
- Pricing

---

# Opportunities

Current competitors leave opportunities in:

- Better onboarding
- Cleaner interface
- Faster setup
- Easier follow-up management
- Cross-industry flexibility
- Affordable pricing for small organizations

---

# Risks

Potential competitive risks include:

- Established competitors with larger budgets
- Feature comparison from customers
- Slow product development
- Customer resistance to switching

---

# Success Strategy

We will not try to build every feature competitors have.

Instead, we will focus on:

- Solving core problems exceptionally well
- Delivering a great user experience
- Listening to customers
- Improving continuously
- Expanding features based on real customer needs

---

# Conclusion

Atlas does not need to be the product with the most features.

It needs to be the product people enjoy using every day.

If customers can replace spreadsheets, paper records, and multiple disconnected tools with Atlas, then we have created real value.
